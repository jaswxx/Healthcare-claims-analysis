Healthcare Claims Trend Analysis 

Project Overview
This project looks at a dataset of healthcare claims to learn more about trends in billing, payments, and claims activity. Key operational and financial trends in the data are highlighted using an interactive Tableau dashboard.
The dashboard focuses on answering several important questions:

- Which claim types are the most expensive?
- Which CPT and ICD codes drive the highest spending?
- Which members account for the largest share of healthcare costs?
- How do billed amounts compare to what insurers ultimately pay?

The objective of this project is to transform raw healthcare claims data into clear visual insights that support financial analysis and operational understanding of claim activity.

Dashboard KPIs
The dashboard begins with several key metrics that summarize the overall dataset:
- Total Billed Amount: $2.04M  
- Total Paid Amount: $1.53M  
- Total Claims Processed: 440 claims  
- Total Members: 100 members  

These KPIs offer a brief overview of the data's general financial scope and healthcare activity.
- The gap between total billed ($2.04M) and total paid ($1.53M) indicates a significant adjustment difference of approximately $510K.
- This difference highlights the impact of insurance negotiations, coverage limits, and claim processing rules on final payments.

Dashboard Sections
Billed vs Paid Amount Trend
This section compares the total amount billed by healthcare providers with the amount paid after claims processing.

Values displayed:
- Total Billed Amount: $2.04M  
- Total Paid Amount: $1.53M  
- Payment Difference (Billed – Paid): $ $510,000   
Insight:  
Billed amounts are often higher than the amounts ultimately paid. This difference can reflect negotiated insurance rates, claim adjustments, or coverage limitations applied during the claims processing process.


Claims Volume Analysis
This visualization tracks the overall number of claims included in the dataset.
Values displayed:
- Total Claims Submitted: 440 claims

Analysing claim volume offers insight into the demand for medical services and helps identify trends in healthcare utilisation.

Key Insights from the Dashboard
Several patterns emerge from the analysis:
- Total billed amounts exceed total paid amounts, reflecting typical reimbursement adjustments in healthcare claims processing.
- A consistent payment gap exists between billed and paid totals, which may result from negotiated insurance rates, coverage limitations, or claim processing rules.
- The dataset includes 440 claims, providing a snapshot of healthcare service utilization.
- Monitoring claim volume trends helps illustrate how healthcare demand contributes to overall costs.

Data Limitations
- The dataset represents a limited sample of claims and may not reflect a full healthcare population.
- Member count and claims activity may not fully capture all utilization patterns.
- Results should be interpreted as exploratory rather than definitive.
